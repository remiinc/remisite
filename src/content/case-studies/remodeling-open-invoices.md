Dan runs a seven-person remodeling company out of his truck and his kitchen table. Kitchens, baths, the occasional addition. No office manager. He is the estimator, the scheduler, and the guy who used to chase payment at 9 p.m.

## The challenge

The Henderson kitchen draw was nine days late. $18,400. The homeowners had approved better fixtures by text on a Friday. The completion photo was in Dan's camera roll from 4:12 p.m. the day they wrapped.

He knew all of that. He also had a bath demo starting at 7 the next morning and no clean way to put the chase together without digging through email, texts, and memory after the kids were in bed. He hated asking twice. He hated guessing what he was allowed to say.

## The solution

Remi watched Dan's invoices, client texts, and job notes. For Henderson, she texted him the balance, the Friday approval, the completion photo reference, and a firm draft.

Dan edited one line. Approved it. Same pattern on eight other open draws that week. Nothing went to a client without his yes.

## The results

$42,000 in open draws came back with proof attached. Nine chases went out on Dan's say-so. Zero went out without it.

Dan still runs the jobs. Remi runs the money loop he used to rebuild from scratch every night.

> "Remi showed me Henderson owed $18,400, showed the text where they approved the fixtures, and let me send the chase from the truck. I still decide. I just don't rebuild the whole week at the kitchen table anymore."
> - Dan, remodeling owner
