Chris owns a five-person painting company. Interiors, mostly. He wins work in person, prices it in the truck at lunch, and used to lose it in silence while the crew stayed busy on smaller jobs.

## The challenge

Tuesday: walkthrough on a whole-house interior. $14,800. Homeowner said they needed a day.

Wednesday: Chris was on another job. Meant to follow up that night.

Friday: the estimate was cold. Not because the price was wrong. Because nobody nudged it while Chris was holding a roller somewhere else. That pattern was eating the good walkthroughs.

## The solution

Remi watched Chris's estimate threads. She caught the quiet $14,800 quote, drafted a short follow-up with the last customer reply attached, and texted it to him for approval.

He sent it from the truck. When the homeowner said yes, Remi drafted the deposit ask for materials. Chris approved that too before anything went out.

## The results

The job closed in 48 hours. Deposit approved before materials. One real job that would have slipped, caught while it was still winnable.

> "I don't need Remi to sell color. I need her to catch the estimate I meant to nudge and put the reply in my hand before the week buries it. That's what happened with that $14,800 interior."
> - Chris, painting company owner
