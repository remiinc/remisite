Jake runs an eight-person roofing company. Storm season, tear-offs, the supply-house run at 6:40 a.m. He is fluent on an iPhone and allergic to software that needs a desk.

## The challenge

His mornings were a second job.

Before the first ladder went up, Jake was supposed to remember which finals were late, which deposit still blocked materials, and which homeowner had approved an upgrade mid-job. The answers lived in email, texts, photos, and QuickBooks. He never got a quiet hour to go hunting.

Assistants that wait to be asked do not help a guy who never sits down.

## The solution

Remi watched completed jobs and invoice status overnight. At 6:12 a.m. she texted Jake a short brief: three overdue finals totaling $28,500, proof on each, drafts ready. One deposit still needed before a material order.

He approved two chases in the truck on the way to the supply house. Left the third for later. Never opened an app to rebuild the list.

## The results

$28,500 in money decisions was waiting before the first site visit. Remi started the day. Jake stayed on the roofs.

> "I don't open five apps to figure out who owes me. Remi texts me the finals, the proof, and the draft before I hit the supply house. I approve what I want. That's it."
> - Jake, roofing owner
