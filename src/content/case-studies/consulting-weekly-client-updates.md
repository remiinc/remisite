A consulting owner was doing client work during the day and rebuilding the business side on Fridays. Remi helped bring overdue retainers, promised next steps, and client update drafts into one place before the weekend started.

## The challenge

The owner knew the client work. The hard part was remembering every business-side thread that came with it.

A client said the second retainer would be paid after a milestone. Another asked for work outside scope in a meeting. A third needed a status update, but the facts were split across email, notes, and project messages. None of it was mysterious. It was just scattered.

## The solution

Remi watched the sources that shaped each account: email, meeting notes, calendar context, project messages, and invoice history.

When a retainer was overdue, Remi drafted a polite chase with the invoice amount and the last relevant client message attached. When a scope question needed the owner, Remi pulled the original words back into view. When a weekly update was due, Remi prepared the draft and showed the sources used.

## The results

Remi surfaced $11,200 in overdue retainers, drafted five client updates, and tied two scope promises back to the source.

The owner still decided what to send. The difference was that Friday started with the money, promises, and proof already waiting for review.

> "I did not need another project tool. I needed someone to remind me who owed us money, what we promised, and what was safe to send back to the client."
> - Founder, consulting studio
