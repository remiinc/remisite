Luis owns a six-person landscaping company. Install days, driveway conversations, crews that keep moving while the paperwork lags behind. He was tired of winning upgrades on site and arguing about them later.

## The challenge

Tuesday, 2:40 p.m. Homeowner Maria points at a bed and asks for more plantings. Luis says yes. $6,200. The price lives in a text. The crew keeps working.

Two weeks later the invoice looks like a surprise. Maria remembers a smaller number. Luis remembers the driveway. The only defense is "you said it on Tuesday," and that is a shitty way to run a business.

Handshake upgrades were turning into unpaid work or ugly conversations. He was done with both.

## The solution

Same day, Remi caught the yes in the text thread and drafted a short confirmation: what Maria approved, the $6,200, and her original words. Luis approved it from his phone before the crew ordered the extra material.

When the final invoice went out, the upgrade was already tied to source. No reconstruction. No he-said.

## The results

$6,200 confirmed in writing the same afternoon. Materials ordered against a real yes. Next invoice did not turn into a dispute.

Remi did not invent the sale. She made the agreement impossible to misremember.

> "Remi caught Maria's yes from the driveway and put it in writing for me to approve before we ordered anything. That one habit has saved me more arguments than any spreadsheet I ever tried."
> - Luis, landscaping owner
