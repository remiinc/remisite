## The situation

A client-service team is managing updates across Slack, email, meetings, and a project tracker. The team is responsive, but the work is scattered. A client ask lands in a shared channel, the owner is implied but not named, and the next step depends on a tracker update from another teammate.

Nothing is broken yet. That is the problem. Client work usually fails quietly before it becomes visible.

## What Remi watches

Remi watches the places where the operational signal already appears, then keeps the source attached to the next step.

- Slack threads with client asks, timing changes, blockers, and scope hints
- Email replies that need a client-safe draft or internal follow-up
- Calendar context for meeting prep and promised next steps
- Tracker references that show owner, status, and due date
- Documents and notes that explain what changed

The goal is not to replace the team. The goal is to make the handoff easier to review.

## What Remi prepares

When the team reviews the open loop, Remi can prepare a small packet:

1. The client ask, with source links
2. The likely owner and due date
3. The latest tracker context
4. A draft client reply
5. A proposed internal reminder if the owner is blocked

The draft is not treated as sent. It is ready to review, with the source context still attached.

## Why it helps

The team gets a clearer view of what is waiting, who owns it, and what needs approval before anything client-visible happens. Remi does the connective work that usually gets done by memory, tabs, and a late-day Slack search.

That means fewer follow-ups have to live in someone’s head.

## What changes next

After review, the team can approve the client reply, adjust the tracker proposal, or assign the reminder. Remi keeps the work grounded in the sources that created it, so the next person can see what changed and why.
