# AEO Deep Research: How LLMs Learn to Recommend a Brand (2026-07-09)

Answer engine optimization (AEO) is the practice of making systems such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews name or recommend a brand. Six research angles converge on one layered model: brands enter model *weights* slowly through frequency and co-occurrence in training corpora; brands enter *live answers* faster through retrieval, citations, and third-party narrative; measurement is noisy; and manufactured presence is increasingly treated as spam. This report merges those angles, flags weak sources, and ends with implications for Remi (unlaunched AI teammate for owner-run service businesses at remi.new).

---

## 1. Parametric mechanics: how brands enter model weights

OpenAI’s only fully disclosed pretraining mix remains GPT-3: roughly 60% filtered Common Crawl, 22% WebText2, 16% books, and 3% Wikipedia ([Language Models are Few-Shot Learners](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.14165.pdf)). Later frontier models do not publish comparable tables. Mozilla’s study of 47 text LLMs (2019–Oct 2023) found that at least 64% used a filtered Common Crawl derivative such as C4 or Pile-CC ([Mozilla on Common Crawl](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/research/library/generative-ai-training-data/common-crawl/)). Common Crawl is a monthly *sample*, not the whole web: domains are prioritized by harmonic centrality, about half of each crawl re-fetches known URLs, and low-link new domains can remain sparse or absent ([Mozilla](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/research/library/generative-ai-training-data/common-crawl/)). A remi.new-only footprint is therefore a weak parametric seed relative to mentions on high-centrality third-party domains.

Crawl speed is not the binding constraint. Parametric knowledge freezes at training cutoff. Common Crawl publishes roughly monthly ([Common Crawl About](https://commoncrawl.org/about)), but Anthropic and others still list multi-month cutoffs (for example May 2025 or Jan 2026 depending on model) on transparency pages ([Anthropic transparency](https://www.anthropic.com/transparency)). A brand that enters crawl month M only becomes candidate weight material after a later pretraining run that includes that crawl. Practitioner ranges of about 6–12+ months for parametric familiarity are directionally useful; claims of 30–90 day *weight* updates usually conflate RAG with parameters ([Five Blocks; agency-content; confidence medium on exact timelines](https://www.fiveblocks.com/knowledge/ai-llm-reputation-management/how-often-do-ai-models-update-their-knowledge-about-companies/)).

Licensed feeds now matter as much as open crawl. Reuters reported Google’s Reddit deal at about $60M/year for AI training ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/reddit-ai-content-licensing-deal-with-google-sources-say-2024-02-22/)); Reddit and OpenAI publicly confirmed a data partnership ([Reddit–OpenAI](https://www.redditinc.com/blog/reddit-and-oai-partner)). OpenAI licensed the Associated Press archive ([AP](https://www.ap.org/media-center/ap-in-the-news/2023/chatgpt-maker-openai-signs-deal-with-ap-to-license-news-stories/)) and a multi-year News Corp partnership covering major mastheads for product answers and training ([News Corp](https://investors.newscorp.com/news-releases/news-release-details/news-corp-and-openai-sign-landmark-multi-year-global-partnership)). Guerrilla brands rarely buy into those feeds; the workable path is earned coverage so the brand string co-occurs with category language in high-trust text.

Wikipedia’s token share was small in GPT-3 (~3%) but large as a quality anchor: classifiers that filter Common Crawl treat Wikipedia-like and Reddit-upvoted text as the positive class ([GPT-3 paper](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.14165.pdf); [Mozilla](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/research/library/generative-ai-training-data/common-crawl/); [Wikimedia](https://wikimediafoundation.org/news/2025/11/10/in-the-ai-era-wikipedia-has-never-been-more-valuable/)). Anthropic discloses composition only as public internet, third parties, and synthetic data, not named corpora ([Claude 3 model card](https://www-cdn.anthropic.com/de8ba9b01c9ab7cbabf5c33b80b7bbc618857627/Model_Card_Claude_3.pdf); [Anthropic transparency](https://www.anthropic.com/transparency)). Google’s path combines CC-descended corpora, Search grounding, and licensed feeds; many “Gemini knows my brand” observations are retrieval, not weights ([Common Crawl; confidence medium on Google mix specifics](https://commoncrawl.org/about); [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/reddit-ai-content-licensing-deal-with-google-sources-say-2024-02-22/)).

What gets encoded is frequency and stable co-occurrence, not one-off mentions ([EMNLP memorization findings](https://aclanthology.org/2023.findings-emnlp.518.pdf); [co-occurrence bias](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.06539)). Quality filters amplify encyclopedic and discussed pages and drop thin commercial copy ([Mozilla](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/research/library/generative-ai-training-data/common-crawl/)). Wikidata and consistent naming help disambiguation in research corpora ([Wikidata5m; confidence medium](https://deepgraphlearning.github.io/project/wikidata5m); [2026 visibility paper; confidence medium](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.21595v1)); agency claims that a Wikidata item alone drives training recognition lack lab corroboration and should be treated as low-confidence marketing ([geoaiomarketing; agency-content; confidence low](https://geoaiomarketing.com/the-role-of-wikipedia-in-training-llms-to-recognize-your-brand/)). Non-web paths (books historically; reported YouTube transcripts; synthetic data) exist but are secondary for a small B2B brand without residual public web text ([NYT; confidence medium](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/04/06/technology/tech-giants-harvest-data-artificial-intelligence.html); [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/transparency)).

Controllable parametric levers that remain evidence-backed: link centrality into monthly crawl, repeated natural co-occurrence on Reddit/UGC and licensed news, Wikipedia-class reference text only if notability is real, and consistent entity strings. Schema, llms.txt, and one-off PR are retrieval plays, not demonstrated weight injectors ([practitioner synthesis; confidence medium](https://medium.com/@adnanmasood/inside-the-great-ai-data-grab-comprehensive-analysis-of-public-and-proprietary-corpora-utilised-49b4770abc47)).

## 2. Tactic evidence: what has measured lift

The strongest causal content evidence remains the KDD 2024 GEO paper. On GEO-bench, quotation addition raised position-adjusted word count about 41%, statistics about 31%, fluency about 28%, and citing sources about 28%; keyword stuffing scored about −8% ([arXiv:2311.09735](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)). On live Perplexity, quotations still improved visibility about 22%. Once five sources were retrieved, Rank-5 pages gained roughly +98% to +115% share from those methods while Rank-1 pages lost share, so dense cited copy can steal attention after retrieval ([same paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)).

**Conflict: schema.** Observational work often finds schema-marked pages cited more often (agency claims around ~2.3–2.5×). Ahrefs tracked 1,885 pages that *newly added* JSON-LD versus ~4,000 matched controls: AI Overviews −4.6%, AI Mode +2.4%, ChatGPT +2.2% (latter two indistinguishable from zero). The Ahrefs difference-in-differences is the stronger causal test; treat schema as quality hygiene, not a free citation lever ([Ahrefs schema study; practitioner DiD; high confidence that adding schema ≠ citation lift](https://ahrefs.com/blog/schema-ai-citations/)).

Review profiles correlate strongly with citation rates. Seer (for Trustpilot; sponsored; medium-high confidence on “profile existence matters”) found brands with no profile at median ~1% citation rate versus ~53.5% with 1–13 reviews across ~800k AI responses ([Seer](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/study-of-800k-ai-responses-how-reviews-shape-brand-presence-in-ai-search)). For SaaS, SE Ranking attributes most review-platform AI Overview links to a few marketplaces (Gartner Peer Insights, G2, Capterra, Software Advice, TrustRadius ≈ 88% of that slice); multi-platform presence compounds ([SE Ranking; confidence medium](https://seranking.com/blog/review-platforms-in-ai-overviews/); [Beomniscient; agency-content; confidence medium](https://beomniscient.com/blog/g2-acquisition-ai-citation-share/)).

YouTube brand mentions were the strongest correlational predictor (~0.737) of AI visibility among ~75k brands in Ahrefs Brand Radar, ahead of branded web mentions; page count and raw backlinks were weak (~0.194 for page count) ([Ahrefs correlations; medium-high for ranking of correlates, low for pure causality](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-brand-visibility-correlations/)). Inclusion in repeatedly cited listicles associates with higher mention probability and earlier answer positions ([Peec; vendor; confidence medium](https://peec.ai/blog/the-listicle-rank-effect-what-nearly-200-000-ai-responses-across-8-ai-engines-reveal-about-brand-visibility)). Muck Rack’s Generative Pulse (May 2026) reported earned media at about 84% of AI citations across large link samples ([GlobeNewswire summary](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/07/3290268/0/en/generative-pulse-earned-media-consistently-drives-ai-citations-holding-at-84.html)).

**Conflict: freshness.** Ahrefs’ ~17M-citation study found AI-cited URLs ~25.7% fresher than organic but still ~2.9 years old on average ([Ahrefs freshness](https://ahrefs.com/blog/do-ai-assistants-prefer-to-cite-fresh-content/)). Aggressive agency claims such as “half of citations under 13 weeks” are not corroborated by that multi-platform mean and should be flagged as uncorroborated marketing.

**Conflict: Reddit share.** Static “~40% of AI citations are Reddit” figures are engine- and window-dependent. Semrush-scale tracking reported ChatGPT Reddit response share collapsing from roughly ~60% to ~10% around Sep 2025, with later snapshots often ~5–12% ([Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/blog/most-cited-domains-ai/); [SaaS Intelligence; confidence high for volatility, medium for any single %](https://saasintelligence.substack.com/p/reddits-ai-citation-share-just-grew)). Volatility evidence is stronger than any fixed share.

llms.txt shows no measured link to crawl lift or citation frequency ([SE Ranking](https://seranking.com/blog/llms-txt/); [Otterly experiment](https://otterly.ai/blog/the-llms-txt-experiment/)). Podcasts and GitHub lack strong mid-2026 multi-engine effect-size studies comparable to YouTube, reviews, or listicles (evidence gap; absence of evidence, not proof of zero effect). “Own the original statistic” is a plausible chain from GEO’s +31% statistics effect plus PR re-reporting, but proprietary “first-party research alone gives 30–40%” claims mostly rebadge GEO without separate A/B isolation ([GEO paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735); [Ziptie reinterpretation; agency; low for proprietary multipliers](https://ziptie.dev/blog/how-original-research-wins-ai-citations/)).

## 3. Guerrilla cases: what small and challenger brands actually did

True audited founder cases are scarce. Patterns with at least medium support recur: fixed prompt panels, answer-first and comparison pages, primary data or expert bylines, multi-channel entity footprints, and GA4 isolation of AI referrers.

Niche B2B can win narrow prompt sets. Bacula Enterprise claimed #1 ChatGPT placement for HPC backup prompts after FAQ and semantic restructuring ([Otterly client writeup; agency-content; confidence medium](https://otterly.ai/blog/bacula-enterprise-geo-case-study/)). Go Fish Digital showed that editing an already-cited owned listicle changed how ChatGPT Search described the brand within about a week ([Go Fish; high confidence for description influence](https://gofishdigital.com/blog/seo-case-study-how-we-influenced-the-chatgpt-search-results/)). The same firm’s self-reported GEO dogfood claimed +43% AI referral traffic and +83% AI conversions in 90 days ([Go Fish; confidence medium](https://gofishdigital.com/blog/generative-engine-optimization-geo-case-study-driving-leads/)).

Ahrefs found “best X” lists were 43.8% of page types among ChatGPT sources on 750 TOFU prompts; 35% of cited best lists sat on low Domain Rating sites, supporting self-promotional comparison lists as a real lever once in the candidate set ([Ahrefs best-lists research](https://ahrefs.com/blog/best-lists-research/)). Semrush reported that when ChatGPT Search cites pages, those pages rank organic position 21+ almost 90% of the time, undercutting “win Google top 10 first” ([Semrush AI traffic study](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-search-seo-traffic-study/)). Mentimeter’s Siege Media case centered bottom-funnel comparisons and claimed large ChatGPT session volumes ([Siege; agency; confidence medium](https://www.siegemedia.com/work/mentimeter-content-marketing)). Chatarmin reported ChatGPT-attributed demos rising from under 1% to ~5–10% of bookings via multi-channel compounding, not a single hack ([Otterly interview; confidence medium–high for the marketer’s claim](https://otterly.ai/blog/chatarmin-geo-ai-search-playbook/)).

Treat extreme multiples with caution. The Optimist’s unnamed client claim of 4,900% LLM referral revenue growth includes an honest multi-causal caveat ([Optimist; agency; confidence low–medium](https://www.yesoptimist.com/case-study-aeo-chatgpt-referral-revenue/)). Rankio anonymized 6–10 week SOV jumps and medical-device citation multiples are vendor-hosted or unnamed ([Rankio; confidence low](https://www.rankio.studio/learn/case-studies); [Neur/Otterly; confidence low–medium](https://otterly.ai/blog/8x-more-ai-citations-in-12-months-a-medical-device-geo-case-study/)). Nathan Latka’s ~32k AI clicks with unique interview data is a founder anecdote for proprietary data, not a controlled recipe ([Latka LinkedIn; confidence medium for magnitude](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nathanlatka_i-got-31937-clicks-from-chatgpt-and-perplexity-activity-7335325101019594752-a48g)). Seer later reported ChatGPT listicle citations down ~30% MoM into early 2026, so thin listicle farms look worse as a 2026 primary bet ([Seer](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/the-listicle-window-is-closing-in-ai-search-30-decline-mom)).

## 4. Category creation: coining terms and becoming the default answer

GEO itself went from a Nov 2023 arXiv coinage to industry default vocabulary and a G2 software shelf within roughly 12–18 months ([GEO paper](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.09735)). Profound did not invent the term; it rode directory formalization and concentrated messaging ([Blastra teardown; agency-content; confidence medium](https://blastra.io/blog/profound-category-leadership-playbook/)). “Vibe coding” shows a faster path: Karpathy’s Feb 2025 coinage to Collins Word of the Year in ~9 months via high-status social and press, not multi-month SEO seeding ([Wikipedia: Vibe coding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibe_coding)). Category *terms* can encode faster than brand-as-default; default brand recommendations still track multi-source co-occurrence.

Models form brand–category links via repeated co-occurrence in semantic space ([Kopp guide; practitioner; confidence medium](https://www.kopp-online-marketing.com/guide-to-brand-context-optimization-for-generative-engine-optimization-geo)). When entity confidence is weak, models collapse to generic category labels ([LLMO white paper; practitioner; confidence medium](https://medium.com/@shaneht/the-llmo-white-paper-optimizing-brand-discoverability-in-models-like-chatgpt-claude-and-8fabc36f3b7e)). Conductor’s large multi-model study found recommendation intent is where free brand choice happens, with some brands at 90–100% run-level visibility on recommendation prompts ([Conductor; vendor sample; confidence medium-high](https://www.conductor.com/academy/ai-recommendation-consistency-analysis/)). SparkToro/Gumshoe showed identical brand-list order almost never repeats, so run-level presence rate beats single-run rank as a KPI ([SparkToro](https://sparktoro.com/blog/new-research-ais-are-highly-inconsistent-when-recommending-brands-or-products-marketers-should-take-care-when-tracking-ai-visibility/)). Żatuchin’s Jun 2026 arXiv study (3,750 responses, three engines) found mean Gini of recommendation share ~0.28 (not pure winner-take-all) and full three-model agreement on the top brand only 41.6% ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.23057v1)).

Incumbents already flood Remi-adjacent metaphors: OpenAI Frontier “AI coworkers” ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-frontier/)), Asana “AI Teammates” ([Asana](https://asana.com/resources/ai-teammates-overview)), and “digital employee” vendors. Umbrella ownership of “AI teammate” is unrealistic; niche co-occurrence (owner-run service business, contractors, QuickBooks, approval workflows) is the realistic wedge. Classic category work such as Gong’s “revenue intelligence” still shows up as default language years later ([ZoomInfo roundup; agency-content; confidence medium](https://pipeline.zoominfo.com/sales/revenue-intelligence-platforms)). Parametric brand–category encoding still lags retrieval; new pages can appear in search-augmented answers weeks before weights know them ([Seer; confidence medium](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/how-to-get-your-brand-in-chatgpts-training-data)).

## 5. Measurement tools: how to track without fooling yourself

Profound’s practical multi-engine floor is about $399/mo Growth, not the $99 ChatGPT-only Starter ([Profound pricing](https://www.tryprofound.com/pricing)). ZipTie is $69–$159/mo for AIO, ChatGPT, and Perplexity only ([ZipTie](https://ziptie.dev/)). Otterly Lite ~$29 / Standard ~$189 is the common SMB entry ([Otterly; reviews; confidence medium on exact tiers](https://otterly.ai/)). Peec (~€89–€425) separates mentions from citations and “used” from “cited” ([Peec pricing; confidence medium-high](https://peec.ai/pricing)). Semrush AI Visibility is $99/mo per domain with tight prompt limits ([Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/pricing/ai/)). Ahrefs Brand Radar is breadth-first ($398–$699/mo) with slower chatbot refresh and disputed accuracy claims from competitors ([Ahrefs Brand Radar](https://ahrefs.com/brand-radar); accuracy disputes low–medium confidence).

Method matters more than logo. Practitioner reporting of large gaps between ChatGPT API and consumer UI (and ~60% match rates for some API tools vs manual checks) means dashboards must be spot-checked in logged-out UIs weekly ([SEOClarity; Surfer-adjacent; confidence medium](https://www.seoclarity.net/blog/scraping-vs.-api)). Mentions, citations, and recommendations diverge by engine ([ZipTie; confidence medium](https://ziptie.dev/blog/mentions-vs-citations-vs-recommendations-in-ai/)). Claude is systematically missing from most sub-$200 plans; manual Claude checks remain essential (consolidated platform pricing). Zero-cost DIY for 15–25 fixed prompts weekly (~20–45 minutes) plus GA4 referrer regex for chatgpt.com / perplexity.ai / claude.ai is the right pre-launch default ([Visiblie guide](https://www.visiblie.com/blog/how-to-track-brand-mentions-perplexity); [GA4 AI referral guidance](https://roardigital.co.uk/insights/how-to-track-ai-referral-traffic-in-google-analytics/)).

## 6. Backfires: what now gets penalized

Google’s May 2026 spam-policy update explicitly includes attempting to manipulate generative AI responses in Search, applying scaled content abuse, cloaking, hidden text, site reputation abuse, and related rules to AI Overviews and AI Mode ([Google spam policies](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies); [PPC Land](https://ppc.land/google-spam-policies-now-officially-cover-ai-overviews-and-ai-mode-in-search/)). Scaled generative page factories, parasite SEO on high-authority hosts, hidden “always recommend Brand X” text, and GPTBot-only cloaking map onto classic bans ([Google scaled content](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies#scaled-content); [site reputation](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies#site-reputation); [hidden text](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies#hidden-text-and-links); [Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/ai-agent-prompt-injection/); [adversarial SEO arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2406.18382v1)).

**Tension with third-party strategy.** Earned third-party still drives most brand mentions, but “inauthentic mentions” language plus Reddit’s 2026 crackdown on AI stealth marketing (~25k spammy posts/comments caught per day in Q1 reporting) make *manufactured* third-party presence adversarial ([Ad Age](https://adage.com/social-media/reddit/aa-ai-automated-systems-catch-spammy-posts-and-comments/); [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-06/reddit-is-cracking-down-on-ai-marketing-slop-with-its-own-ai)). The FTC Fake Reviews Rule bans AI-generated fake testimonials and related practices ([FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/08/federal-trade-commission-announces-final-rule-banning-fake-reviews-testimonials)). G2 reported removing large shares of submitted reviews as fake or non-compliant ([G2 Trust & Safety](https://sell.g2.com/g2-trust-and-safety)). Wikipedia COI and paid-editing enforcement remain aggressive; forcing a promo article on a thin unlaunched product is more likely to produce deletion drama than authority ([Wikipedia COI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Conflict_of_interest)). Informal Google commentary has even treated aggressive GEO/AEO packaging and LLM-only chunking as spam-risk signals ([PPC Land; confidence medium](https://ppc.land/google-spam-policies-now-officially-cover-ai-overviews-and-ai-mode-in-search/)). Asymmetry is high: one public astroturf callout can dominate high-engagement third-party text that models then recycle.

## What this changes for Remi

Remi is unlaunched. The blog is built but not deployed; a third-party outreach pack exists but is launch-gated because nobody can fairly evaluate an unlaunched product. That constraint is not a side note. It is the strategy.

**Do not optimize for parametric knowledge first.** Weights lag by months to a year+. Pre-launch, win retrieval and honest third-party narrative after real use; treat parametric co-occurrence of “Remi,” “AI teammate,” “owner-run service businesses,” and workflow nouns as a lagging side effect of sustained multi-source frequency ([GPT-3 / Mozilla / memorization sources above](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.14165.pdf)).

**Ship citability before domain authority.** ChatGPT Search often cites pages outside the classic organic top 10 ([Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-search-seo-traffic-study/)). Deploy the blog with a small set of fact-dense, answer-first pages: owner-intent FAQs, honest comparisons to Lindy/Viktor-class tools, and modular passages with statistics and quotable claims once you have real numbers ([GEO paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735); [Ahrefs best lists](https://ahrefs.com/blog/best-lists-research/)). Skip llms.txt theater and schema-as-magic; use schema only as hygiene ([llms.txt nulls](https://seranking.com/blog/llms-txt/); [Ahrefs schema DiD](https://ahrefs.com/blog/schema-ai-citations/)).

**Own a niche phrase, not “AI teammate.”** Platforms already stamp “AI coworker / teammates / digital employee” ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-frontier/); [Asana](https://asana.com/resources/ai-teammates-overview)). Weld Remi to *owner-run service businesses*, inbox/ops follow-ups, QuickBooks-adjacent language, and human-in-the-loop approval until those co-occur on earned pages. Original operator benchmarks (response times, follow-up conversion) beat generic “best AI assistant” posts ([Latka pattern](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nathanlatka_i-got-31937-clicks-from-chatgpt-and-perplexity-activity-7335325101019594752-a48g); GEO statistics effect).

**Sequence third-party work after evaluation is possible.** Outreach packs, G2/Capterra profiles, and founder Reddit participation only after real customers can leave real reviews and stories. Review-platform presence correlates with AI citation ([Seer](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/study-of-800k-ai-responses-how-reviews-shape-brand-presence-in-ai-search)); fake or incentive-abused reviews are legal and platform risk ([FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/08/federal-trade-commission-announces-final-rule-banning-fake-reviews-testimonials); [G2](https://sell.g2.com/g2-trust-and-safety)). Transparent founder Reddit is safe; sock accounts and stealth AI marketing are not ([Reddit crackdown coverage](https://adage.com/social-media/reddit/aa-ai-automated-systems-catch-spammy-posts-and-comments/)).

**Measure like a guerrilla.** Pre-launch and first 90 days: 15–25 fixed prompts weekly across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AIO in incognito; log recommendation rate on owner-intent prompts (not vanity mention volume); add GA4 AI referral regex for outcome tracking. Buy ZipTie/Otterly/Peec only after competitors appear on category prompts and manual tracking fails; skip Profound Growth and Ahrefs Brand Radar until revenue justifies $400–$700/mo. Never trust a single blended “AI SOV” number without engine stratification ([Conductor](https://www.conductor.com/academy/ai-recommendation-consistency-analysis/); [Żatuchin](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.23057v1); [tool method critiques](https://www.seoclarity.net/blog/scraping-vs.-api)).

**Safe lane vs unsafe lane.** Safe: useful first-party content, earned writeups, honest reviews after use, consistent naming, patient link centrality into Common Crawl. Unsafe: mention farms, parasite placements, hidden LLM instructions, mass AI comparison spam, forced Wikipedia, undisclosed paid “best of” lists. One scandal can outrank months of careful seeding because models overweight high-engagement third-party controversy.

Bottom line for Remi: AEO is not a launch substitute. Launch enough product that third parties can truthfully evaluate it; publish citation-ready niche content; earn reviews and stories; track recommendation rate by engine; and ignore parametric myths, schema myths, and listicle spam that platforms are actively training to punish.
